"A land developer worked out where excess cut could go — around wetlands, stream buffers, and skimmer basins — using area, distance, stockpile volume, and cut/fill to design, all in one exhibit on the environmental-compliance overlay. Multi-tool earthwork Land dev PM
Quantifying a fill shortfall behind homes
Korge 20-acre Feb 2026
Talking point
"A PM quantified a fill shortfall against the design — about 8,200 cubic yards of net import needed — and backed it with elevation checks and a cross section, all in one public exhibit. The contractor opened the link and had the number and the locations without a site visit or a call."

Value delivered

Replaces a field visit, a separate sketch, and a follow-up call. The contractor opens the link and sees the quantified shortfall (−8,172 yd³ against design), the low areas, and the cross-section profile — enough to scope the import and the work.

What they did

Combined point elevation checks, a cross section, and a site-wide cut/fill balance (Cut 4,891 yd³ / Fill 13,063 yd³ / Balance −8,172 yd³) to document the deficiency. Named it "FILL NEEDED BEHIND HOMES" and made it PUBLIC so the field team could see it immediately.

Tools used

Point elevation Cross section Cut/fill balance

Grading alternatives analysis across ~50 lots
Leon Ridge Mar 2026
Talking point
"A civil engineer evaluated three layouts for adding a block of lots — each quantified by pad-removal group and street, one option totaling nearly 67,000 cubic yards. Three named options, three exhibits, all from the same drone scan. That's an alternatives analysis without a separate survey or a Civil 3D session per option."

Value delivered

A formal grading alternatives analysis directly from drone data — no separate survey, no rebuilding a Civil 3D model per option. The three exhibits are the deliverable: comparable, already quantified (~66,862 yd³ for Option 3), shareable internally.

What they did

Saved three named option exhibits across a block of ~50 lots. Option 3 breaks the work into pad-removal groups (lots 26–33, 34–49, 50–62) and street earthwork — with cut to design, fill to design, and an estimated volumes table totaling 66,862 yd³.

Stockpile placement Land dev PM
Defining exact stockpile zones with measured setbacks
Trillium May 2026
Talking point
"A land developer drew the exact stockpile zone — 75 feet off the access road, 30 feet off the trees, capped at 5 feet — and shared it with the crew. The contractor places it right the first time instead of building it in the wrong spot and moving it."

Value delivered

A stockpile placed too close to the road or trees — or built too high — has to be relocated. That's double-handling the material, a direct cost. Defining the zone with measured setbacks and a height cap up front prevents the misplacement and re-handling.

What they did

Defined the permitted placement zone using distance and area, then annotated constraints directly on the map: 75' road setback, 30' tree setback, 5' height cap, and specific materials instructions for the field crew. Made PUBLIC for immediate crew access.

Tools used

Distance ×2 Area ×2 Annotations

Recurring lot grade verification across a whole community
The Woodlands Hills May 2026
Talking point
"A land developer is checking lot grade and slope on individual lots against the engineered design — six elevation points and a cross section, right on the CAD overlay. And it's not a one-off: it's a numbered series they run across the whole community for grade and erosion monitoring."

Value delivered

Reading lot grade from the drone scan plus the design overlay replaces sending a crew to shoot grades on individual lots. Maintaining a numbered exhibit series builds the ongoing record — for both grade verification and erosion-control monitoring across the full development.

What they did

Placed 6 elevation points and a cross section on lots 47–48 over the engineering CAD overlay. Part of a recurring "TWH-##" numbered series maintained across the community — companion exhibits also track erosion and SWPPP monitoring alongside grade checks.
